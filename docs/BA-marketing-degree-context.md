BA (Hons) Marketing & Business Management (R84) – Syllabus Analysis and Accelerated Plan
Executive Summary: The OU R84 Marketing & Business Management degree is a 360‑credit BA (Hons) divided into three stages (360 credits total
). Stage 1 covers core business and marketing fundamentals (e.g. B100: Introduction to Business & Management – 60 credits
, B128: Marketing Essentials – 30 credits
, plus one 30‑credit option). Stage 2 focuses on organizational integration and customer insight (B207: Shaping Business Opportunities – 60 credits
; B206: Understanding Customers – 60 credits
). Stage 3 is strategic and applied marketing (B302: Strategic Management – 60 credits
; B328: Marketing in Action – 30 credits
; B323: Brands, Branding & Society – 30 credits, planned 2027). Each module includes tutor-led support and continuous assessment (TMAs and EMAs). For example, B100 (introductory OU level 1, 60 credits) has 5 tutor-marked assignments + an end-of-module assessment
 and covers the nature of organizations, core functions (HR, accounting, marketing), and external context including ethics
. B207 (level 2, 60 credits) includes 3 TMAs, 1 computer-marked CMA, and an EMA
, covering innovation, globalisation, and long-term strategy
. B206 (60 credits, level 2) examines consumer and B2B behavior through psychology and cultural lenses
.

Gaps vs. 6-Month Goals: The OU curriculum provides a solid theoretical grounding, but lacks some modern practical skills needed for a 6‑month accelerated program. Notably, it omits hands-on digital tools and AI: there is no module on digital marketing tools (e.g. Google Analytics, SEO/SEM platforms, social media ads, CRM systems) or emerging AI capabilities. While ethics are covered generally (B100 Block 6 on business ethics
), data privacy and consent (e.g. GDPR, first-party data) are not explicitly taught. OU covers analytics basics (B126 Business Data Analytics), but not marketing-specific measurement architecture or attribution modelling. Executive communication skills (presenting ROI to C-suite, dashboards) are touched on in LB170 (Communication) and in project work, but not as core module outcomes. Gaps in Accelerated Curriculum vs. OU Syllabus
The OU syllabus is comprehensive at a theoretical level but lacks several practical areas needed for an accelerated, hands-on marketing program:

Modern Marketing Tools & AI: OU does not teach specific platforms (Google Analytics, Ads, CRM systems, etc.) or AI-based content tools. It focuses on concepts, not on actual software or current digital practices.
Analytics & Measurement Architecture: OU covers statistics (M140) and data analytics (B126), but not marketing analytics (customer journey analytics, conversion tracking, dashboard creation, marketing performance modeling). There is no mention of attribution models or real-time metrics.
Marketing Operations: The curriculum has no dedicated module on marketing operations. OU covers operations in B207 broadly, but does not teach how to set up marketing processes, campaign management, or MarTech stacks in practice.
Attribution/Incrementality: The syllabus does not address measurement models like multi-touch attribution or marketing mix modeling (MMM). Marketing in OU is more analog/strategic, lacking digital-first metrics.
Privacy & Consent: OU includes business ethics (B100 Block 6)
, but little on data privacy, GDPR, or advertising regulations. Modern marketers need to understand legal issues around data collection and AI usage.
Executive Communication: Soft skills exist (LB170), but there’s no explicit training on telling the marketing story to executives or producing board-level dashboards. OU mentions integration with finance/HR, but not the communication skills to align with a CEO/CFO perspective.
These gaps suggest adding new content on digital marketing analytics, AI tools, marketing ops, data privacy, and C-suite communication to meet the accelerated six-month goals.

Additional Topics & Skills to Add
To cover the gaps above, the following modules and micro-skills should be added to the front-end curriculum (in bulleted form):

Digital Marketing Tools: Hands-on training in tools like Google Analytics 4, Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager, SEO platforms (Ahrefs/SEMrush), content creation tools (Canva, Figma), CRM/email tools (HubSpot, Klaviyo) and their AI features.
AI in Marketing: Basic prompting and use of generative AI (GPT, Jasper) for market research, content generation, ad creation. How to use AI for analytics (insight extraction from data).
Measurement Architecture: How to implement analytics: tagging plan, data layers, conversion pixels. Attribution models (first-click, last-click, algorithmic) and A/B testing. Dashboard creation with business intelligence tools.
Marketing Operations: Building a MarTech stack (CMS, CRM, ERP integration). Project management and workflow for campaigns (content calendars, briefs, agile marketing). KPI dashboards and metric tracking.
Privacy & Ethics in Digital Marketing: GDPR, CCPA compliance, cookie consent, ethical AI use (data bias, transparency). Case laws or standards (e.g. ICO guidelines). Responsible data stewardship.
C-Level Communication: Interpreting marketing metrics in financial terms (ROI, CLV, CAC). Storytelling frameworks (e.g. elevator pitch for campaigns). Designing executive dashboards. Presentation skills for executives, including slide design and brevity.
Each of these becomes a “micro-module” in the accelerated program, often mapped to the practical project work below.

Project Ladder: 10 Progressive Briefs
Below are ten project briefs, increasing in complexity. Each project includes deliverables, success criteria, tools, estimated time, and assessment rubric highlights.

Brand & Market Profile (Easy)
Deliverable: One-page profile of an existing brand (choose consumer or B2B), covering mission, target segments, value proposition, and 4Ps. Include a SWOT.
Success: Clear articulation of brand and audience; reasonable SWOT; visuals or mood board; use of at least one tool (e.g. Figma/Canva).
Tools: Canva, Google Slides. Time: ~5–8 hours.
Assessment: Completeness (brand elements covered), insight into positioning, clarity of writing, visual appeal.

Customer Persona & Journey Map
Deliverable: A detailed customer persona (demographics, goals, pain points) plus a visual journey map with key touchpoints (awareness → purchase → loyalty).
Success: Persona is research-backed (even if hypothetical), journey highlights influences (e.g. social media ad, website visit, email).
Tools: Figma (journey diagram), Notion or Word for persona. Time: ~8–10 hours.
Assessment: Realism of persona, logic of journey steps, use of marketing psychology, quality of diagram, connection to marketing mix.

Landing Page Audit & Redesign
Deliverable: Critique of an existing landing page (identify issues: unclear value prop, no social proof, etc.), then wireframe/redesign a better version. Include proposed headlines and CTAs.
Success: Audit identifies at least 3 issues; redesign addresses them using marketing principles; justifications given (conversion rate reasons).
Tools: Figma (wireframe), Hotjar (if possible to analyze example heatmap), Canva. Time: ~10–12 hours.
Assessment: Depth of audit (metrics or UX rationale), effectiveness of redesign, adherence to branding, persuasive copy.

Analytics Tracking Plan
Deliverable: Design a Google Analytics 4 & Tag Manager plan for a sample website: list of key events (e.g. sign-ups, button clicks), GA4 properties, GSC integration, and reporting dashboard mockup.
Success: Clear list of tracked events aligned to business goals, correct use of GA4 structure (property, data streams), sample dashboard screenshot with example KPIs.
Tools: Google Analytics Demo account, Looker Studio (mock-up), Google Search Console (dummy data). Time: ~10–12 hours.
Assessment: Technical correctness, alignment of metrics to objectives, visual clarity of dashboard.

SEO Content Strategy
Deliverable: Select a topic/keyword (with data from Ahrefs/SEMrush), outline a cluster of content (3–5 article titles), and write one page of a content brief (including keyword usage, meta tags, headings).
Success: Keywords are well-researched (search volume vs competition), content plan covers subtopics logically, brief is SEO-optimized (uses chosen keyword, search intent).
Tools: Ahrefs or Google Keyword Planner, Word/Notion for brief. Time: ~8–10 hours.
Assessment: Quality of keyword selection (cited data), coherence of content map, SEO optimization in brief.

Email Campaign Flow
Deliverable: Build an email/SMS flow in Klaviyo (or Mailchimp): e.g. welcome series or abandon-cart sequence. Include at least 3 messages and a segment/trigger setup.
Success: Logical sequence (right messages, timing), personalization (e.g. first name, recommended products), goal metrics (open rate, click rate projections).
Tools: Klaviyo (free trial) or Mailchimp. Time: ~10–15 hours.
Assessment: Relevance of emails to customer stage, use of data/personalization, copy quality, how results would be measured.

Paid Campaign Build
Deliverable: Create a mock Google Search and Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ad campaign for a product. Include audience targeting, ad copy (with A/B variations), budget allocation.
Success: Audience choices make sense (interests/demographics), ads follow platform best practices, budget fits goals. Use of ad preview tools.
Tools: Google Ads interface (draft mode), Meta Ads Manager (preview), Canva (for images). Time: ~12–15 hours.
Assessment: Alignment of targeting to persona, creativity of copy/creative, realistic budget and bid strategy.

Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) Audit
Deliverable: Using Hotjar (or site analytics), identify 3–5 usability issues on a site (or mock site) and propose A/B test ideas to fix them (e.g. simplify form, change CTA color).
Success: Issues are evidence-based (screenshots/heatmaps), tests are sensible (one change at a time), expected improvement estimated.
Tools: Hotjar recordings/heatmaps, GA4 conversion report. Time: ~12–15 hours.
Assessment: Insightfulness of identified issues, A/B test design quality, understanding of UX principles.

Marketing Dashboard
Deliverable: Design a marketing dashboard pulling data from multiple channels (simulate with screenshots if necessary). Show KPIs like traffic sources, conversion rate, CAC, CLV, revenue.
Success: Dashboard is clean and highlights top metrics, with clear labels. Includes at least one chart per channel (e.g. pie chart of traffic sources, trendline of leads).
Tools: Google Data Studio (Looker Studio), or Tableau/Sheets. Time: ~10–12 hours.
Assessment: Relevance of chosen KPIs to business goals, visual clarity, ability to derive insights (story from the dashboard).

Capstone: End-to-End Marketing Plan
Deliverable: A comprehensive plan for a real or hypothetical business (choose one): includes market analysis, SWOT, target segments, positioning, 4Ps strategy for a quarter, integrated media mix (earned/paid), budget, timeline, and KPIs. Also prepare a 10-min C-level presentation (slides).
Success: Plan is cohesive (each part flows logically), aligned to business objectives, includes metrics for ROI evaluation. Presentation is polished (clear visuals, executive summary slide).
Tools: All relevant (Figma/Canva for visuals, Notion/PPT, Google Analytics sample data). Time: ~20+ hours (over 2–3 weeks).
Assessment: Strategic insight, practicality of plan, professional communication, data-driven thinking, creativity. (Rubric: Strategy 30%, Execution plan 30%, Metrics/rationale 20%, Presentation 20%.)
